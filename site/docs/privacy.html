Privacy policy

Track History (trackhistory.app) is a free personal track-day logbook operated by Speedshift LLC. This policy explains, in plain language, what data the app collects and what happens to it. Effective July 15, 2026.

The short version: your logbook is private to your account, there are no ads, no third-party analytics, and your data is never sold. Video and telemetry files are parsed in your browser and never uploaded. The only thing ever made public is a share page you explicitly turn on.

What we collect

  • Google sign-in details. Signing in with Google gives us your Google account ID, email address, name, and profile-picture URL — the standard openid email profile scopes and nothing more. We never see your Google password and can't access anything else in your Google account.
  • What you log. The tracks, events, sessions, lap times, notes, goals, car and conditions details you enter, and any share slug you choose. This is stored so the app can show it back to you.
  • Cookies. A session cookie keeps you signed in, and a short-lived cookie protects the sign-in flow itself. There are no advertising or analytics cookies.
  • On your device only. Your theme preference is kept in browser storage, and if you install the app, pages and recently viewed data are cached on your device so the logbook works offline. That cache never leaves your device.

Telemetry and video files

Telemetry import — Corvette PDR and GoPro video, Racelogic VBO, Garmin FIT — runs entirely in your browser. Videos are read by byte range and files are never uploaded to our servers. The only thing stored is what you choose to save at the end of an import: the resulting sessions and lap times, as ordinary logbook entries.

What we don't do

  • No advertising, and no third-party analytics or tracking scripts.
  • We never sell or rent your data.
  • We don't share your data with anyone, except the cloud hosting provider that stores it so the service can run, or if the law requires it.

Who can see your logbook

Your logbook is private to your account. The one exception is a share page, which you create yourself under a slug you choose: it shows stats and best times but strips notes, your email, and per-lap data, and you can disable it at any time.

Where your data lives

Your logbook is stored in a database run by our cloud hosting provider and is accessed only to operate the service.

Retention and deletion

Your data is kept for as long as you use the service. You can delete individual tracks, events, sessions and laps in the app at any time. To have your account and all of its data deleted, open an issue on GitHub or contact the maintainer. You can also revoke Track History's access to your Google account from your Google account settings.

Children

Track History is not directed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version is posted here with a new effective date.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Open an issue on GitHub.
