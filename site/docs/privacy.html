Privacy policy

Track Evolution (trackevolution.app) is a free personal track-day logbook operated by Speedshift LLC. This policy explains, in plain language, what data the app collects and what happens to it. Effective July 21, 2026.

The short version: your logbook is private to your account, there are no ads, no third-party analytics, and your data is never sold. Video and telemetry files are parsed in your browser and never uploaded. The only thing ever made public is a share page you explicitly turn on.

What we collect

  • Sign-in details. Signing in with Google gives us your Google account ID, email address, name, and profile-picture URL — the standard openid email profile scopes and nothing more. Signing in with Apple gives us your Apple account ID, your name (only on the first sign-in), and your email address — which can be Apple's private relay address if you choose Hide My Email. We never see your Google or Apple password and can't access anything else in those accounts.
  • What you log. The tracks, events, sessions, lap times, notes, goals, car and conditions details you enter, the vehicles in your garage — including the consumable parts, costs, wear measurements and per-day setup sheets you record for them — and any share slug you choose. This is stored so the app can show it back to you. Garage and setup data is never included in share pages.
  • Location, only while recording laps. The mobile app's lap recorder uses your phone's GPS during a recording you start yourself — see location data below for exactly what stays on the phone and what is saved.
  • Cookies. A session cookie keeps you signed in, and a short-lived cookie protects the sign-in flow itself. There are no advertising or analytics cookies.
  • On your device only. Your theme preference is kept in browser storage, and if you install the app, pages and recently viewed data are cached on your device so the logbook works offline. That cache never leaves your device.

Telemetry and video files

Telemetry import — Corvette PDR and GoPro video, Racelogic VBO — runs entirely in your browser. Videos are read by byte range and files are never uploaded to our servers. The only thing stored is what you choose to save at the end of an import: the resulting sessions and lap times, plus the telemetry-derived extras the app shows — the best lap's GPS racing line and per-lap channel data (speed, RPM, lateral G) — as ordinary logbook entries. The recordings themselves are never stored.

Location data (recording laps in the mobile app)

The iPhone/Android app can record a track session's laps from your phone's GPS. Location is collected only while you are actively recording — you start and stop it yourself — and, because the phone is usually stowed while driving, recording continues while the app is in the background. The GPS trace is kept on your phone while you record and until you save or discard it. Saving uploads only the derived results — lap times, the best lap's racing line, and per-lap speed — as ordinary logbook entries, the same as a telemetry import; the fix-by-fix recording itself is never uploaded and is deleted from the phone once saved or discarded. The app never collects your location outside of a recording, and you can revoke location permission at any time in your phone's settings.

What we don't do

  • No advertising, and no third-party analytics or tracking scripts.
  • We never sell or rent your data.
  • We don't share your data with anyone, except the cloud hosting provider that stores it so the service can run, or if the law requires it.

Who can see your logbook

Your logbook is private to your account. The one exception is a share page, which you create yourself under a slug you choose: it shows stats and best times but strips notes, your email, and per-lap data, and you can disable it at any time.

Where your data lives

Your logbook is stored in a database run by our cloud hosting provider and is accessed only to operate the service.

Retention and deletion

Your data is kept for as long as you use the service. You can delete individual tracks, events, sessions and laps in the app at any time. To have your account and all of its data deleted, open an issue on GitHub or contact the maintainer. You can also revoke Track Evolution's access to your Google account from your Google account settings, or to your Apple account from account.apple.com under Sign in with Apple.

Children

Track Evolution is not directed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version is posted here with a new effective date.
