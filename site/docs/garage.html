Garage & setup notebook

The paper notebook and the parts spreadsheet, folded into your logbook: track what's on the car and what it's set to, and let your logged events tell you when consumables will need replacing and what a setup change did to your times.

Vehicles and their garage pages

Your garage lives in Settings → Vehicles. When an event's Car field matches a garage vehicle by name, the event links to that vehicle — that link is what lets parts accrue wear and setups carry over between events. Each vehicle gets a garage page with its track hours, consumables, spend, and a track-hours ledger of every event it ran.

Track hours — the wear clock

Everything wear-related runs on on-track hours, counted automatically from your events: 2 hours per track day by default, which is a realistic amount of green-flag seat time for an HPDE day. If a day ran long or short, set the exact hours on the event's edit form ("On-track hours") and that event uses your number instead. If you log complete lap data, an event's logged driving time is used when it exceeds the estimate.

Consumables and wear projections

Add parts — brake pads, tires, rotors, brake fluid, oil — to a vehicle with their install date, cost, and optionally an expected life in track hours. From then on wear accrues by itself as you log events. Each part shows a wear bar and a projection of what's left, from the best information available:

  • Measured — log a quick pad-thickness or tread-depth measurement between events (two or more), and the app fits your actual wear rate per track hour and projects when you'll hit the replace-at value. This is the number to trust.
  • Expected life — with no measurements, remaining life is expected hours minus accrued hours. When you retire a set and add its replacement, the replacement's expected life defaults to what the old set actually lasted.

Parts due (or nearly due) for replacement surface as a maintenance-due strip on your dashboard — "front pads, about 1.5 track days left" before your next event, not after it. Tires also count heat cycles (one per event day), and retired parts keep their history: hours lived, cost, and cost per track hour.

The setup notebook

Every event carries one setup sheet per day: tire pressures (cold and hot, all four corners), camber, toe, caster, damper clicks, sway-bar settings, fuel, and which pad and tire sets were on the car. Sheets copy forward — a new sheet pre-fills from your previous day (or the car's last event), so you only edit what changed. Values that changed from the previous day are highlighted.

Setup vs. lap times

Each track page ends with a Setup vs. lap times table: every sheet you've logged at that track in date order, what changed between sheets, and the event's best time and consistency alongside. The app deliberately doesn't claim causation — conditions, traffic and driver all move lap times — but it does the recall perfectly, which is the part a paper notebook is bad at.

Private by design

Garage data — parts, wear, spend, and every setup sheet — is never included in share pages. Share your pace; your damper clicks stay yours.
