Getting started

Track Evolution lives at trackevolution.app. Sign in with your Google account and you get your own logbook — this page walks through logging your first event, importing telemetry, and sharing your progress.

Sign in

Open trackevolution.app and sign in with Google. There's no separate account to create — a fresh, empty logbook is waiting on first sign-in, and everything you record is private to your account.

Log your first event

An event is a day (or weekend) at a track. Create one, pick the track and layout, and add the sessions you ran with their lap times. Lap times are entered as m:ss.fff (e.g. 2:03.118).

Only kept a best time? That's fine — every event takes an optional manual best time, so spreadsheet-era history can go in as-is and full lap data can be attached later. The track pages chart your best lap per event over time, per track and layout, so improvement trends downward.

Import telemetry

On any event page, Import video / telemetry… turns recordings into sessions with laps: Corvette PDR video, GoPro footage, and Racelogic VBO files. Parsing happens entirely in your browser — videos are read by byte range and never uploaded. See Telemetry import for the full details, including the start/finish line picker.

Install it on your phone

Track Evolution is an installable web app: use your browser's Add to Home Screen / Install option and it opens like a native app. Static pages and recently viewed data keep working offline, so you can browse your logbook in the paddock. On a touch screen, pull down from the top of any page to refresh your data.

Share your progress

You can publish a read-only page of your stats and best times under a slug you choose (trackevolution.app/share/<your-slug>). Notes, your email, and per-lap data are stripped — share the pace, keep the diary private. See share pages for what's included.
