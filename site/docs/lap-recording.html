Recording laps with your phone

No camera, no lap timer, no hardware — the Track Evolution app for iPhone and Android can record a session straight from your phone's GPS. Start recording before you head out, lock the phone and stow it, and stop back in the paddock: your laps are timed from the trace and saved to the event.

How it works

  1. Start. On the event page, tap Record laps with your phone, then Start recording. The first time, the app asks for location permission.
  2. Drive. Lock the phone and put it anywhere in the car — recording continues in the background with the screen off. Nothing to look at, nothing to tap. On Android a notification shows while recording; on iPhone the status bar indicates location use.
  3. Stop. Back in the paddock, open the app and tap Stop & review laps. You'll see the track map you actually drove — tap where the start/finish line is, and every pass across it becomes a lap, exactly like a telemetry import.
  4. Save. Check the lap list, give the session a label, and save. The session gets lap times, the speed-painted racing line of your best lap, and per-lap speed graphs.

What to expect from phone GPS

Phones report their position about once a second, and crossings of your start/finish line are interpolated between fixes — lap times come out within roughly ±0.2–0.5 s and are shown with a ~ prefix, like all GPS-derived laps. That's plenty for spotting trends across a day or a season; for tenth-accurate timing, import a proper logger's recording instead.

Built to not lose your session

  • The recording is checkpointed on your phone every few seconds — if the app is closed or the phone dies, everything up to that moment is recovered the next time you open the event.
  • Stopping never discards anything: an unsaved recording stays on the phone until you review and save it or discard it. And saving works even with no paddock signal — the session is stored on your phone and uploads automatically when you're back online.
  • If you forget to stop, recording ends itself after the car has been parked for a while — it won't run all day in your pocket.
Your GPS trace stays on the phone until you save. Saving uploads only the derived results — lap times, the best lap's racing line, and per-lap speed — to your logbook, the same as a telemetry import. See the privacy policy.
